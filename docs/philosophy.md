# philosophy

The premise behind revxr is simple: most "dead" tokens are not permanently
dead, they are illiquid and unattended. The scanner's job is to tell you
whether there is anything worth doing about it. The builder's job is to make
the mechanical parts of doing something about it less error-prone.

Revival is not resurrection. A token that has lost its holders, its LP and
its community probably should not come back. But a token whose team walked
away while a few thousand holders remained is a different story, and a
legible scoring pass helps distinguish the two.

Three rules:

1. **Never sign for the user.** The builder returns unsigned transactions.
2. **Score, don't prescribe.** The scanner returns numbers, not advice.
3. **Stay RPC-agnostic.** Snapshots in, scores out. Fetching is the caller's
   problem.
